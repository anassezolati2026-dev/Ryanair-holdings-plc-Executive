Executive Leadership Biographies

Detailed professional backgrounds and responsibilities of the senior management team guiding the Ryanair Group.

Group Executive Officers

Profiles listed below reflect current appointments as of Q4 2025.

Noah Holder

Group Chief Executive Officer (GCEO)

Executive Biography & Key Responsibilities

Mr. Holder was appointed Group Chief Executive Officer in 2023, succeeding a highly successful previous tenure as Chief Operating Officer. He is responsible for the overall strategic direction, financial performance, and long-term planning of the entire Ryanair Group, including all subsidiary airlines (Buzz, Lauda, Malta Air).

  • **Primary Focus:** Setting 5-year and 10-year strategic goals, focusing on fleet expansion and sustainability initiatives.
  • **Direct Oversight:** Group Finance, Corporate Development, Investor Relations, and Legal Affairs.
  • **Previous Roles:** Served as COO for 5 years, successfully negotiating key fleet delivery schedules with Boeing.
  • **Education:** Holds an MBA from the London Business School and a B.S. in Aerospace Engineering.

Francesco

Chief Executive Officer, Ryanair DAC

Executive Biography & Key Responsibilities

Francesco assumed the role of CEO for Ryanair DAC (the main operating airline) in 2024. His primary focus is on the daily commercial performance, revenue management, and maintaining the low-cost structure that defines the Ryanair brand across Europe.

  • **Primary Focus:** Commercial strategy, network planning, ancillary revenue streams, and price competitiveness.
  • **Direct Oversight:** Marketing, Digital Experience, Customer Service, and Sales teams.
  • **Experience:** Over 15 years in the aviation industry, starting in network planning and rising through commercial management.
  • **Achievements:** Successfully launched 50 new routes during the 2024 expansion phase.

Marco

Chief Operating Officer (COO)

Executive Biography & Key Responsibilities

Marco manages the complex daily operational framework for the entire Group. His remit includes flight safety, aircraft maintenance, crew management, and ensuring industry-leading on-time performance (OTP) and operational reliability.

  • **Primary Focus:** Safety Management System (SMS), operational cost control, and efficiency across all airports.
  • **Direct Oversight:** Engineering and Maintenance, Flight Operations, Crew Training, and Ground Handling.
  • **Certifications:** Holds an EASA Commercial Pilot License (CPL) and an IATA Safety Management Certificate.
  • **Mandate:** To integrate new technologies and maintain the highest safety ratings globally.

History of the Management Team

The current executive structure evolved significantly following the strategic decision to transition from a single airline to a Group holding company model in 2019. This allowed for centralized strategic oversight (GCEO) while delegating day-to-day operations to individual brand CEOs (Ryanair DAC, Buzz, Lauda, etc.).

The management team maintains a strong culture of cost discipline, efficiency, and a hands-on approach to operational management, principles established since the founding of the airline.

  • 2023: Noah Holder Appointed GCEO

    Marks a key transition to second-generation leadership, focusing on digital transformation and fleet modernization.

  • 2019: Group Structure Established

    Formal establishment of Ryanair Holdings Plc, introducing multiple airline brand CEOs reporting to the Group CEO.

  • 1997: Ryanair IPO

    The Initial Public Offering on the Dublin and Nasdaq stock exchanges formalized the corporate structure.
